TEAM INSIGHTS

// HACKER

Working on the literature review gave me a stronger grasp of the technical foundations required for our system — especially in web-based development and data handling. Understanding what makes current systems effective sharpened my thinking on building something dependable.

// HIPSTER

The literature study revealed how deeply UI and UX shape the effectiveness of feedback systems. Simplicity and accessibility are not optional — they're what motivate people to actually speak up. That insight drove every design choice I made.

// HUSTLER

The literature review reframed how I think about adoption. Responsiveness and transparency aren't nice-to-haves — they build the trust that makes a system actually get used. That became the foundation of how I approach pitching and presenting our work.

// HANDLER

Structure is what makes a system survive contact with reality. By examining how other projects were organized and what went wrong, I learned to oversee our project more deliberately — keeping every component aligned with our goals.
